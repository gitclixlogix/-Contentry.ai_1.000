## Executive Summary

This document audits Contentry.ai's messaging approach and provides recommendations for positioning to Right Management. The core message should emphasize **reputation risk prevention** through content governance, not just content creation efficiency.

## 1. Core Messaging Framework

**Current:**

## 7. Messaging Don'ts

### 7.1 What NOT to Claim

| Don't Claim | Why | Say Instead |
|-------------|-----|-------------|
| "Reputation management platform" | We don't monitor external mentions | "Reputation risk prevention" |
| "Crisis management" | No real-time monitoring | "Crisis communication support" |
| "Social listening" | Not implemented | "Social publishing with governance" |
| "Competitor tracking" | Not implemented | Don't mention |
| "100% compliance guarantee" | AI isn't perfect | "Compliance risk reduction" |

### 7.2 Honest Limitations to Acknowledge

| Limitation | How to Address |
|------------|----------------|
| No social listening | "We focus on what YOU publish, not what others say" |
| Fixed role system | "Granular permissions coming soon" |
| No email notifications | "In-app notifications with email integration planned" |
| No crisis detection | "We're proactive prevention, not reactive monitoring" |

## 9. Summary

### Key Messaging Principles

1. **Lead with governance, not generation** - Differentiation is in oversight
2. **Quantify risk prevention** - Make compliance tangible
3. **Address stakeholder-specific pain** - Different messages for different roles
4. **Acknowledge limitations honestly** - Build trust through transparency
5. **Position against alternatives** - Clear competitive differentiation
