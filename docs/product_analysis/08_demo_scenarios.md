## Scenario 1: Layoff Communication Management

### 1.1 Business Context

**Situation:**
A Right Management client (GlobalTech Inc.) is conducting a reduction in force affecting 200 employees across North America and Europe. Right Management is advising on communications to protect the employer brand during this transition.

**Challenge:**
- Multiple communications needed (affected employees, remaining employees, managers, media)
- Legal compliance critical (no discriminatory language, proper disclosures)
- Cultural adaptation for European audiences
- Multiple stakeholders must approve (Client HR, Legal, CEO)
- Timeline: 1 week to prepare all communications

### 1.2 Demo Flow

**Step 1: Show the Challenge (1 min)**
> "Imagine you're advising a client on layoff communications. One wrong word could mean a lawsuit. One culturally insensitive phrase could damage their employer brand in Europe. How do you ensure everything is perfect?"

**Step 2: Create Strategic Profile (2 min)**
- Navigate to Strategic Profiles
- Show existing "GlobalTech Transition Communications" profile
- Highlight:
  - Tone: Empathetic, transparent, professional
  - Audience: Employees at all levels
  - Guidelines: Legal compliance requirements
  - Knowledge base: Company policies, severance info

**Step 3: Generate Layoff Communication (2 min)**
- Click "Generate Content"
- Input prompt: "Create an empathetic letter to employees being laid off at GlobalTech Inc. Include information about severance, outplacement services, and next steps. The letter should be professional, compassionate, and legally compliant."
- Show AI generation in progress
- Review generated content

**Step 4: Show Compliance Analysis (2 min)**
- Click "Analyze"
- Show scoring dashboard:
  - Overall Score: 82/100
  - Compliance Score: 78/100 ⚠️
  - Cultural Sensitivity: 85/100
  - Accuracy: 92/100
- **Highlight flagged issue:**
  - "The phrase 'long-tenured employees' could be interpreted as age-related. Consider using 'experienced employees' or removing the phrase."
- Show recommendation to fix

**Step 5: Submit for Approval (1 min)**
- Click "Submit for Approval"
- Show approval workflow: Creator → Legal → HR → CEO
- Demonstrate notification to approvers

### 1.3 Key Messages During Demo

| Point | Message |
|-------|---------|
| AI Generation | "AI creates the first draft in seconds, not hours" |
| Compliance Check | "This caught a potential discrimination issue BEFORE it went to legal" |
| Workflow | "All stakeholders review in a coordinated workflow" |
| Audit Trail | "Every approval is logged for legal protection" |

## Scenario 2: Multi-Stakeholder Approval Workflow

**Situation:**

**Challenge:**
- Multiple approvers in different time zones
- Need visibility into approval status
- Must track who approved what and when
- Cannot publish until all approvals received

- Show list of pending items

## Scenario 3: Cultural Sensitivity for Global Communications

### 3.1 Business Context

**Situation:**
GlobalTech Inc. operates in 12 countries. The same layoff message needs to be communicated in the US, Germany, Japan, and Brazil. Each region has different cultural expectations.

**Challenge:**
- Same message, different cultural contexts
- Avoid cultural missteps that damage brand
- Ensure appropriate tone for each region
- Maintain core message consistency

### 3.2 Demo Flow

**Step 1: Show US Version Analysis (1 min)**
- Open US layoff communication
- Show Cultural Sensitivity Score: 88/100
- Expand Hofstede dimensions:
  - Power Distance: 85 ✓
  - Individualism: 90 ✓
  - All dimensions green

**Step 2: Analyze Same Content for Germany (2 min)**
- Change target region to Germany
- Re-analyze content
- Show Cultural Sensitivity Score drops: 72/100 ⚠️
- Highlight issues:
  - Uncertainty Avoidance: 58 ⚠️
    - "German audiences prefer more specific details about process and timeline. The phrase 'next steps will be communicated soon' is too vague."
  - Power Distance: 65 ⚠️
    - "Consider more formal salutation for German professional context."

**Step 3: Analyze for Japan (1 min)**
- Change target region to Japan
- Show score: 65/100 ⚠️
- Highlight issues:
  - Individualism: 52 ⚠️
    - "The message focuses on individual impact. Japanese audiences respond better to collective framing."
  - Power Distance: 58 ⚠️
    - "Consider expressing regret from leadership more prominently."

**Step 4: Show Adapted Version (1 min)**
- Generate Japan-adapted version
- Show improved score: 86/100 ✓
- Highlight changes:
  - Added collective language ("our team", "together")
  - Prominent leadership regret statement
  - More detailed process explanation

### 3.3 Key Messages During Demo

| Point | Message |
|-------|---------|
| Global Risk | "The same message can be perfect in the US and offensive in Japan" |
| Hofstede Framework | "We use an academically validated cultural framework" |
| Actionable Feedback | "We don't just flag issues - we explain why and how to fix" |
| Adaptation | "AI can adapt the message while keeping the core meaning" |

### 3.4 Demo Data Needed

```

Cultural Analysis Scores:
- US: 88/100 (all green)
- Germany: 72/100 (Uncertainty Avoidance, Power Distance flags)
- Japan: 65/100 (Individualism, Power Distance flags)
- Brazil: 78/100 (minor Indulgence flag)

Adapted Versions:
- Germany version with specific timelines
- Japan version with collective framing
- Brazil version with more personal warmth
```
